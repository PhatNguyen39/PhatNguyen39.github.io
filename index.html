Research

★ ACL 2025 · System Demonstrations

SlimLM: An Efficient Small Language Model for On-Device Document Assistance

Thang M. Pham, Phat T. Nguyen, Seunghyun Yoon, Viet Dac Lai, Franck Dernoncourt, Trung Bui

Proc. of ACL 2025 (Vol. 3: System Demonstrations), pp. 436–447 · Vienna

A series of small language models (125M–8B params) optimized to run fully on-device on a Samsung Galaxy S24 for document tasks — summarization, QA, question suggestion. We map the size / context-length / latency trade-offs for real smartphone deployment, cutting server cost and improving privacy. Published in the ACL 2025 System Demonstrations track; work done with Adobe Research.

About

I'm an ML engineer who likes the full stack of the work — from model internals to a working system someone can actually use. I build ML pipelines end to end: feature engineering, training and tuning, and serving models behind real interfaces.

My research sits where NLP meets financial systems: how small models behave on-device, how multi-agent systems reason about markets, and how far we can trust an LLM to evaluate another LLM. Co-authoring SlimLM — published at ACL 2025 — gave me hands-on depth in efficient model design and real-device deployment.

I hold an M.S. in CS (Machine Learning) from Georgia Tech (4.0 GPA) and an MBA in Finance — so I can move between PyTorch and the financial question a model is actually meant to answer.
